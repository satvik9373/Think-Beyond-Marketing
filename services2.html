Frequently Asked Question

Google Ads marketing is a form of online advertising where businesses pay to display their advertisements on Google’s search engine results pages (SERPs), YouTube, and across the Google Display Network. Advertisers bid on keywords that users might search for, aiming to attract potential customers to their websites by displaying targeted ads.

Google Ads operates on a pay-per-click (PPC) model, meaning you only pay when someone clicks on your ad. Advertisers bid on keywords relevant to their products or services, and Google determines which ads to show based on factors like bid amount, ad quality, and relevancy. This process ensures that your ads reach users actively searching for related products or services.
